SF Bay Area Renters' Federation

What is this?

Have you ever thought to yourself, "I wish the Bay Area could build more housing units"?
US TOO. As it turns out, every week there are opportunities for you, dear member of the public, to weigh in on whether specific projects should be built. Join our googlegroup to be alerted of opportunities to tell decision makers all over the Bay that it's time to get going with housing production.
"Uhh, can't I just vote on something?" Sure, put your contact info and street address in the form below. We will email you when it is time to vote. SIGN UP NO MATTER WHERE YOU LIVE, WHETHER OR NOT YOU ALREADY REGISTERED TO VOTE. Elections happen everywhere, and you have until October to register.

Southside Spirit House

In anticipation of the California Young Democrat's Convention in Anaheim, the San Francisco Young Democrats are hosting a pre-convention mixer of San Francisco Democratic clubs on Tuesday May 12th from 6-9pm at Southside Spirit House in SOMA. We will have a reserved space as well as drink specials. Participating organizations include the Sf Young Democrats, Black Young Democrats of SF, SF Tech Dems, South Beach Democratic Club, College Democrats at SFSU, SF Bay Area Renters' Federation, and others.

200 4th St. community meeting

May 12, 6:30PM

520 3rd Street, Oakland, CA, United States, Suite 109

Carmel Partners, a Bay Area-based infill real estate development firm, and Jack London District-based KTGY Architects are proposing a new 330 residential unit mixed use housing project on a one-and-a-half block site at 200 4th Street in the Jack London District. The site is located between Jackson and Madison Streets, and 3rd and 5th Streets, and currently has the one-story CostPlus offices on the full block site, and a surface parking lot on the half block site. This is the second community meeting on the project, following a lively community kick-off meeting in March and follow-up meetings with key community groups.
