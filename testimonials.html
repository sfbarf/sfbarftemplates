Brian Hanlon

I support SFBARF because I don’t want to see San Francisco become a boutique city for the rich. My initial involvement with housing politics in SF consisted of attending anti-eviction protests and organizing meetings, but eventually, I grew frustrated that anti-eviction and neighborhood groups neglected to address the root-cause of the eviction crisis. Decades of under-development coupled with a tech-fueled jobs boom are the main causes of the housing crisis, not Airbnb or “speculators.” I support strong tenant protections and applaud the direct action work of anti-eviction activists, but this crisis will only worsen unless San Francisco and all Bay Area municipalities drastically increase housing production. We need hundreds of thousands of new housing units in the Bay Area and we need them now. SFBARF is fighting to make the Bay Area affordable for everyone by agitating to create massive amounts of new housing at all price levels.
